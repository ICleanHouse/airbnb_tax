## Accountability

The project owner is the sole accountable person for the Stage 1 owner,
engineering owner, pilot operator, feedback lead, backup operator, and
privacy/legal-review roles. This intentionally records the absence of
independent backup and independent review. It does not claim that the owner is
an independently qualified lawyer or privacy professional.

Paid experiments remain deferred. Before public signup is enabled, the project
owner must approve the privacy notice, consent boundaries, retention/deletion
rules, and survey processor. Any qualified external review obtained later must
be linked to the release evidence.

## Approved charter

- Use this priority order: marketplace liquidity, trust/retention, learning
  speed, operational simplicity, business-plan readiness, then early recurring
  revenue.
- Keep the documented free core unchanged through M0–M5. Do not select or build
  a payment provider, commission, final subscription price, billing,
  platform-handled cleaning payments, or paid experiment during Stage 1.
- Limit the Stage 1 launch and observation cluster to Sofia.
- Treat Sofia hosts managing approximately 1–20 short-term-rental properties as
  one primary Stage 1 segment. Do not give portfolio hosts automatic priority
  over hosts with 1–3 properties.
- Include agencies as a first-class launch role. Full launch-critical agency
  parity—account/profile, member invitations and membership, eligible job
  access, applications/offers, assignment, immutable member delegation,
  notifications, verification, and history-preserving recovery—must pass the
  release gate before the marketplace launches. Advanced agency tooling remains
  evidence-gated after launch.
- Record that no compliant existing user or lead list is available.
- Defer candidate interviews and desk-based competitor research. Preserve the
  agreed future comparison set: direct turnover workflows, adjacent operations
  SaaS, home-services marketplaces, travel marketplaces, payment providers as
  context only, and substitutes such as phone, messaging, spreadsheets,
  referrals, and social groups.
- Treat `docs/monetization/action_plan.docx` as background only unless a specific
  assumption receives a later owner decision.
- Use four evidence labels for Stage 1 reporting: **fact**, **assumption**,
  **recommendation**, and **decision**.

## Launch and validation contract

- Public account creation may open only after the pre-launch privacy review.
  Real marketplace work remains locked until Gates A–C and the applicable
  verification and supply-readiness requirements pass.
- Before live marketplace access, hosts, cleaners, agency accounts, and
  delegated agency members must have both verified email and verified phone,
  an active approved account, and complete role-specific onboarding.
- A role-ready host has a complete Sofia profile and at least one complete Sofia
  property. A role-ready cleaner has a complete profile, service area, and
  availability. A role-ready agency has a complete profile/service area and at
  least one separately role-ready active member cleaner.
- At full marketplace launch, every eligible Sofia job is available under the
  existing role, privacy, status, timing, and object-authorization rules. There
  is no invite-only job cohort and no operator-release requirement.
- The Stage 1 North Star is **role-ready activated users**, reported separately
  for hosts, cleaners, and agencies. Registrations, operationally successful
  jobs, assignment, completion, repeat use, reliability, incidents, and
  operator effort remain required supporting measures.
- Stage 1 uses descriptive reporting rather than numeric pass/fail thresholds.
  Raw counts, denominators, exclusions, unknown outcomes, role splits, and
  counter-evidence must still be reported. The absence of a threshold must
  never be presented as proof of success.
- The first formal Stage 1 readout occurs 90 complete days after full
  marketplace launch and selects Proceed, Extend once, Pivot, or Stop through a
  recorded owner decision.

## Evidence collection and surveys

- Use necessary first-party operational records for accounts, profiles, jobs,
  applications, assignments, notifications, support, disputes, reviews, and
  audit history.
- Optional behavioral analytics require explicit consent before collection.
  Do not interpret “collectable” as permission for unbounded collection.
- Candidate interviews are not part of this Stage 1 cycle. Actual users may
  receive an optional post-signup survey.
- Survey outreach is admin-initiated only. The minimum capability filters by
  role, activation, city, and activity, then requires explicit manual recipient
  selection and an audience preview.
- Survey invitations use localized in-app and/or email delivery through the
  durable notification system and link to an approved external survey.
  Responses remain outside the application. The survey processor, notice,
  consent, access, retention, and deletion rules require pre-launch approval.
- No participant incentives or survey compensation are approved.
